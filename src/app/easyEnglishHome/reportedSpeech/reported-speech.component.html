Reported Speech

Reported speech, also known as indirect speech, is used to report what someone else has said. It is commonly used in storytelling, interviews, and conversations. In reported speech, the tense, pronouns, and other elements may change to reflect the speaker's original words. There are two main types of reported speech: statements and questions. Statements are used to report statements or facts, while questions are used to report questions asked by someone else.
